Anshul Lau

(Founder / Chief Executive)

Starting my career in the Indian Army, I learned the importance of discipline and resilience. After a career change due to injury, I earned two Masters’ Degrees in the UK and found my calling in the digital technology field. For over 12 years, I have worked across numerous universities such as Manchester, Cambridge, Nottingham, and Hertfordshire, focusing on enhancing the digital side of education. My dedication to the service and uniform continued as I joined the British Army Reserve, further honing my leadership and strategic skills.

With an international reputation as a consultant on higher education reform, I have advised governments and academic bodies, bringing to the table a rich expertise of cross-disciplinary insights. I have been instrumental in driving digital learning initiatives, leading institutions to enhance education through technology. With a portfolio that spans across significant educational technology projects, I have been a key figure in projects amounting to strategic advancements and collaborations within the academic sector. I bring a unique blend of strategic military discipline and creative academic insight to my current endeavours, which include pursuing advanced training in Data Science and AI from the UK’s Alan Turing Institute.

A certified Lego Serious Play Facilitator, I have mastered the art of applying playful creativity to problem-solving and collaborative team-building exercises. My academic credentials, coupled with ongoing professional development, position me as a leader and innovator in digital education, shaping the future of learning with a blend of strategic vision and technological acumen.

Dr Swati Virmani

(Chief Pedagogy Officer)

I am a Senior Lecturer and Teacher Fellow at De Montfort University, UK. I was recently also appointed as the Associate at the UK Economics Network. I am an experienced and innovative educator with a proven track record of teaching excellence, curriculum development, and research. I have extensive expertise developing curricula at both undergraduate, postgraduate, enterprise levels. I have spearheaded the integration of new topics such as Economics of AI into core modules, ensuring students graduate with relevant and contemporary knowledge. My student-focused and engaging teaching style, incorporating game-based learning, experiential activities, and technology, has earned me teaching awards including the Economics Network's ‘Best New Lecturer in the UK’ award in 2021.

My research aims to bridge the gap between academia and industry, focusing on AI and its impact on professions, building inclusive smart cities, and digital strategy building. As a member of the CIPR’s AI in PR (Public Relations) panel, I have worked on three major research reports, analysing the use of AI tools in PR, assessing the profession's preparedness for an AI future, and emphasising the practical support required for upskilling. My most recent work also includes the public engagement project funded by the Alan Turing Institute (UK) to work with people from different backgrounds and underrepresented groups to expand public knowledge and unbiased information around data science and AI.

I am passionate about pushing the boundaries of education through funded research and connecting with partners globally. With my extensive experience leading programmes and developing innovative curricula, I am well-prepared to lead the enhancement of education delivery in alignment with institutional strategy.
